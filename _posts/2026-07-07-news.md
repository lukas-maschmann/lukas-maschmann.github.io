I am happy to share that our paper **“Cultural Origins of Preventive Health Care Utilization”**, joint with Jan Bietenbeck, Therese Nilsson, and Devon Spika, has been accepted in the *Journal of Economic Behavior & Organization*.

In the paper, we study whether culturally transmitted time and risk preferences help explain differences in preventive health care use. Using data on second-generation immigrants in Europe, we find that descendants of more patient cultures are more likely to use preventive health services, while descendants of more risk-taking cultures are less likely to do so.

The current version of the paper is available [here](https://docs.iza.org/dp18301.pdf).
